Geography

Bandung lies in a highland basin encircled by volcanic hills in West Java, about 700 to 760 meters above sea level. The city spreads from a historic core along Asia Afrika and Braga toward northern slopes like Dago, with rivers such as Cikapundung cutting through the urban fabric.

Climate

The elevation gives Bandung a mild tropical highland climate with cooler temperatures than coastal Java, warm afternoons, and crisp mornings and evenings. Rain is heaviest from about November to April, while the midyear months are generally drier.

Culture

andung mixes Sundanese traditions with a strong modern creative scene shaped by universities and design schools. Art deco heritage, music, fashion, and community arts festivals remain prominent, and everyday culture emphasizes friendliness, student life, and pride in the city’s role in Indonesia’s independence and the 1955 Asian African Conference.
